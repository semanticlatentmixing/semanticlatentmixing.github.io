Abstract

In this paper, we tackle the problem of domain adaptation of image generators. Given a pretrained image generator, the aim is to adapt it, without training on new images, to generate images belonging to new keyword-defined domains. We leverage the power of CLIP models for image-text similarity and the disentangled representation found in the latent spaces of generative adversarial networks (GANs). Rather than finetuning pretrained generators, we frame the problem as mapping one latent space to another. We propose training a latent-to-latent mapper (L2L) to map a large number of CLIP text directions to the latent spaces of GANs. This allows the mapper to model the both the interrelationships between text embeddings and the relationship between the latent spaces. Consequently, our method obtain higher quality results in a fraction of the training time and parameters of the state-of-the-art methods. Furthermore, our method enables test-time composition of keywords and even extends to some unseen words. We further demonstrate the generalizability of our method by showing results on different kinds of style-based GANs.
