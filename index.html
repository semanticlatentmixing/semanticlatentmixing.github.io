Abstract

We propose a training-free method for textual editing of real images using diffusion models. Diffusion models are advantageous for inversion since they operate on noised images or spatial latents. However, previous methods encode real images in textual tokens thus requiring optimization or finetuning. We propose leveraging the spatial image latent for preserving image details. We show how the diffusion process is amenable to spatial guidance using a reference image. First, we propose aligning latents conditioned on textual prompts to remain faithful to the input image. Second, we demonstrate the ability of diffusion models to understand the semantics of real images based on aligned latents. Third, we improve results by semantically mixing the aligned latent with an unrestricted latent. Owing to the full utilization of the advantages of diffusion models, our method is significantly faster than prior methods since no finetuning of the diffusion model or costly embedding via optimization is required. Additionally, unlike previous approaches, our method does not require storing large finetuned models. We believe this gives our method a competitive advantage for editing on mobile devices and for applications that require faster editing results. We show extensive results to demonstrate the qualitative and quantitative performance of our method.
